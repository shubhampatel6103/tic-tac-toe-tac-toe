Overview

The board is a 3×3 grid of 3×3 tic‑tac‑toe boards (9 small grids total). Win small grids to claim big squares; align three claimed big squares in a row to win the game.

  • • Big-board win: three big squares in a line claimed by the same player.
  • • If no line wins and all big squares are decided, the side with more claimed big squares wins; equal → draw.
Big Board Structure

Each big square contains a small 3×3 tic-tac-toe board.

How Turns Work

  1. You play an X in one of the allowed small-grid cells inside a valid big square.
  2. Your cell’s row/col sends the opponent to the big square at that same (row, col).
  3. If that target big square is already decided (won/draw), the opponent may play in any in-progress big square.
  4. Win a small grid by making three-in-a-row inside it; it becomes claimed by X/O and no longer playable.
In this example, O played in cell (2,2) of big square (0,0), sending X to big square (2,2) for their next turn.

How You Win

  • Claim a small grid to mark its big square with X/O.
  • Form three claimed big squares in a row, column, or diagonal to win.
  • If all big squares are decided and no line win exists: the side with more claimed big squares wins; equal → draw.

Tips

  • Think two boards ahead: the cell you play determines your opponent’s next big square.
  • Prefer sending the opponent to big squares where you’re strong or they’re constrained.
  • Control the macro center and build two-in-a-row threats across big squares.
